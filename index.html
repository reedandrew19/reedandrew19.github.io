ABOUT

I’m currently a software engineer working at Lockheed Martin in Camden, New Jersey, creating in house tools to increase efficiency for the Ship Integration and Testing branch. I’ve spent much of my life jumping between hobbies, never sure which to pursue as a career, until I found programming. Whether reading through assembly language to create a Pokémon skill for the Amazon Echo, or creating a chromatic tuner with an Arduino, computer science has since become a helpful supplement to various parts of my life.

I enrolled in the computer science program at Moravian College after I fell in love with an electronics class that integrated programming. The Moravian computing department encouraged engagement in and out of lecture halls. I’ve competed in programming competitions, was the secretary of the computing club, and took part in three internships, and was a TA and tutor. Though I have taken classes in databases, networking, and have worked on websites (admittedly, my worst and least favorite subject), my favorite topic has to be optimizing algorithms.

I believe that learning everything about a given language is not as important as mastering the steps required to perform a calculation. Because of this, I like to focus on big ideas rather than memorizing libraries; I like learning about design patterns, functional programming, and code clarity. I’m particularly passionate about code clarity and automated testing because of how much I enjoy optimization both inside and out of code. It has been said that 80% of a coder’s job is in maintenance of code. If this is the case, then easily read code whose functionality can be verified quickly alleviates the majority of the problems a programmer faces.
