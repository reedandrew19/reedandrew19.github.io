ABOUT

I’m currently a software engineering intern at The Jackson Laboratory in Bar Harbor, Maine, creating ways to present data to assist lab faculty with their research as well as help outside parties get involved with the Jax mission. I grew up in Downeast Maine (which I only began to appreciate after I left), went to high school at the Maine School of Science and Mathematics in Limestone, Maine and spent my freshman year of college at Simmons College in Boston where I fell in love with the city, but I felt the learning environment was ill-fitted to my needs as a student. It was then that I transferred to Moravian College where I graduated in the Spring 2017.

Over the past three years at Moravian, I’ve been trained in development, taking classes in several sections of the computer science discipline. Meanwhile, I have taken several graphic design and web design courses since I’ve always had a natural affinity for design, thus it was a no-brainer for me to start web development and design. It was a perfect combination of design for my right brain and logic for my left brain.

Even though I thought that web design and development was where I wanted to be, I also found a passion for data science. Data science is a completely different beast, but it’s AWESOME. Most of my data-related experience has been with healthcare data, but it’s opened my eyes to the huge potential that big data has that scientists have only really begun to unlock.
